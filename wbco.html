Medical-grade, explained

Why professional skincare is different.

Medical-grade (or “cosmeceutical”) skincare sits between a high-street moisturiser and a prescription treatment. The difference isn’t the packaging — it’s the formulation.

Drug-store skincare is formulated for everybody and no one at the same time: active percentages are kept low so they can’t cause a reaction on any skin type. That keeps them safe to sell, but it also means results are slower, softer, and often invisible.

WBco is built around clean but effective actives — prebiotics, salicylic acid, balanced oils, hydrating botanicals — in delivery systems that suit real skin. That’s why you’ll usually find these products through a trained skin professional: they work best when someone who knows your skin picks them for you.

Paired with in-clinic treatments — anti-wrinkle, fillers, skin boosters, polynucleotides, microneedling — good homecare is what keeps your skin looking like you’ve just left the clinic, for longer.
